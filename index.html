Sales Data Walmart Analysis

Project Overview

This project entailed the creation and management of a sales database for Walmart, focusing on extensive data manipulation and analysis to derive actionable insights. Key tasks included database and table creation, data cleaning, and the addition of calculated columns such as time_of_day, day_name, and month_name to enrich the dataset. Advanced SQL queries were utilized to explore data dimensions like product line performance, sales trends by time and city, and customer behavior analysis, including gender-based preferences and payment methods. The project highlighted unique insights into sales patterns, product popularity, and customer engagement, providing a robust foundation for strategic business decisions.

Techniques used:

  • Database and table creation with MySQL for structured data storage.
  • Complex SQL queries for data cleaning, transformation, and analysis.
  • Insights into sales trends, customer demographics, and product performance.
  • Strategic analysis of tax implications, revenue by product lines, and customer types.

This comprehensive analysis not only showcased my ability to manipulate and analyze large datasets but also to derive meaningful insights that could drive business strategy and improve operational efficiencies.

Vehicle CO2 Emissions Analysis

Project Insights

This analysis project delved into seven years of Canadian vehicle CO2 emissions data to uncover insights related to environmental impact. Key findings include the median engine size, fuel consumption rates across different fuel types, and the correlation between fuel consumption and CO2 emissions. The analysis also compares CO2 emissions between 'SUV - SMALL' and 'MID-SIZE' vehicle classes, revealing which class is more environmentally friendly.

Key insights from the project:

  • The median engine size in liters across all vehicles.
  • Average fuel consumption for regular, premium gasoline, ethanol, and diesel.
  • Positive correlation between fuel consumption and CO2 emissions.
  • Comparison of average CO2 emissions between 'SUV - SMALL' and 'MID-SIZE' classes.
  • Average CO2 emissions for all vehicles and specifically for those with an engine size of 2.0 liters or smaller.

The project highlighted the critical role of vehicle type and fuel consumption in CO2 emissions, providing valuable insights for environmental impact reduction strategies.
